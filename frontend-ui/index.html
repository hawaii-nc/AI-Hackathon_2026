The problem

Meeting someone in crisis is the easy part.

Then comes the maze. Dozens of organizations, each with different eligibility rules and capacity that changes by the day. Finding the one that fits — today, for this person — can take hours of phone calls. And every wrong turn costs something that's hard to win back: their trust.

Different eligibility

Veteran-only. Women-only. Sober-only. The rules rarely match the person standing in front of you.

Shifting capacity

Beds open and close by the hour. Yesterday's answer is already wrong by the time you call.

No shared map

The network lives in one worker's memory. When they're out, the knowledge walks out the door too.

The shift

Kōkua reads the whole situation — and finds the fit.

From a photo of handwritten notes or a few typed lines, Kōkua tags what matters — veteran status, clinical needs, housing, eligibility, proximity — then scores compatibility against every nearby resource. Ranked, with the reasoning shown, so the worker stays in command.

How it works

Three steps. The worker decides every one.

01

Describe

Snap a photo of your handwritten notes or type a few lines. Kōkua turns it into structured needs.

02

Match

A compatibility model ranks nearby organizations by how well they truly fit — not just who has a bed.

03

Refer

Approve an AI-drafted introduction email. You always review before anything is sent.

The point

Marcus has a bed tonight.

The right match, made in minutes, by the worker who actually knows him. That's the whole product — and it's ready for Hawaiʻi today, built to scale to every county after.
